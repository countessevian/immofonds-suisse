The market is beyond your control. But saving money on taxes isn’t.

Consider it a savvy investor’s secret: When a stock dips in value, it can be an opportunity to save money on taxes. Regardless of the market conditions, we can take advantage of stocks that are down to help lower your tax bill. And the less you owe in taxes, the more you could take home in earnings.

part 1

Invest in S&P 500® stocks.

With S&P 500 Direct, you’ll own individual stocks (including whole and fractional shares) that make up the S&P 500® index—some of the largest companies driving the US economy. By owning hundreds of stocks—instead of a single ETF—we’ll help find more opportunities to collect tax savings and lower your tax bill.

part 2

Save money on taxes.

As the market moves, we’ll look for opportunities to turn day-to-day losses into tax savings. At tax time, these savings can help lower your taxes on capital gains and ordinary income—and any losses you don’t use in a given year could translate into future tax savings.

Invest in stocks of the S&P 500®. Except the stocks you don’t want.

Some stocks you may not want to own. Maybe a stock doesn’t align with your values or you don’t need more equity in your employer. Or maybe you just need to be mindful of certain trading restrictions. Unlike an ETF, you can opt out of any stock in the S&P 500® while you continue to track the index. Just opt out your unwanted stocks when you open your account and we’ll automatically rebalance the weights to keep you in line with the index.
